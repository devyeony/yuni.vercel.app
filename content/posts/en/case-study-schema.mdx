---
title: A schema that refuses vague case studies
summary: >-
  Portfolio case studies drift into feature lists. Mine can't — the content
  pipeline rejects any project that doesn't state its problem, trade-offs,
  and measured outcomes.
date: "2026-07-18"
tags:
  - portfolio
  - content
  - zod
---

Every portfolio says "built X with Y." Almost none say what the problem was,
what the alternatives cost, or what changed because the thing shipped. Not
because people don't know — because nothing forces them to write it down.

While rebuilding this site I made that forcing function structural. Case
studies live in MDX, but the frontmatter is validated by Zod at build time,
and the schema requires the three things a case study is actually for:

```ts
schema: z.object({
  problem: z.string().min(1),
  decisions: z
    .array(z.object({ decision: z.string().min(1), tradeoff: z.string().min(1) }))
    .min(1),
  outcomes: z.array(z.string().min(1)).min(1),
  // …title, period, per-role contributions, stack, links
})
```

The interesting field is `decisions`. Each entry must carry a `tradeoff` —
you cannot record a decision here without admitting what it cost. "We used
JPA" doesn't compile. "We introduced JPA alongside MyBatis, accepting two
persistence idioms to avoid freezing feature work on a rewrite" does.

The pages just render this structure. Which means the discipline survives
me: any future project I add — or any AI agent adds on my behalf — goes
through the same gate. If the trade-offs aren't written, the build is red.

Schemas are usually pitched as protection against bad data. This one is
protection against bad writing.
